# Transformer Problem Solution

## Problem
A transformer is used to step down the voltage from **120 V AC** to **9.0 V AC**.  
If the primary coil has **400 turns**, how many turns must the secondary coil have?

---

## 📌 Given Values

- Primary voltage:  
  \( V_p = 120 \, V \)

- Secondary voltage:  
  \( V_s = 9.0 \, V \)

- Number of turns in primary coil:  
  \( N_p = 400 \)

- Number of turns in secondary coil:  
  \( N_s = ? \)

---

## ⚡ Transformer Formula

For an ideal transformer, the relationship between voltage and number of turns is:

\[
\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}
\]

---

## 🔄 Rearranging the Formula

We want to find \( N_s \), so we rearrange:

\[
N_s = N_p \cdot \frac{V_s}{V_p}
\]

---

## 🔢 Substituting Values

\[
N_s = 400 \cdot \frac{9.0}{120}
\]

---

## 🧮 Calculation

\[
\frac{9.0}{120} = 0.075
\]

\[
N_s = 400 \cdot 0.075 = 30
\]

---

## ✅ Final Answer

\[
\boxed{N_s = 30 \text{ turns}}
\]

---

## 💡 Interpretation

- Since voltage is **stepped down** (120 V → 9 V),  
  the number of turns also decreases.
- This confirms the transformer is a **step-down transformer**.
- The secondary coil has **much fewer turns** than the primary coil.

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

Voltage and number of turns are **directly proportional**:

- More turns → Higher voltage  
- Fewer turns → Lower voltage  

---

## 🎯 Summary

| Quantity | Value |
|--------|------|
| Primary Voltage | 120 V |
| Secondary Voltage | 9.0 V |
| Primary Turns | 400 |
| Secondary Turns | **30** |
