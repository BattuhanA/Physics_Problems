# 🔌 Transformer Problem Solution

## 📘 Problem

A transformer is used to step down the voltage from **120 V AC** to **9.0 V AC**.
If the primary coil has **400 turns**, how many turns must the secondary coil have?

---

## 📌 Given Values

* 🔋 Primary voltage: Vp = 120 V
* ⚡ Secondary voltage: Vs = 9.0 V
* 🌀 Number of turns in primary coil: Np = 400
* ❓ Number of turns in secondary coil: Ns = ?

---

## ⚙️ Transformer Formula

For an ideal transformer:

Vp / Vs = Np / Ns

---

## 🔄 Rearranging the Formula

We solve for Ns:

Ns = Np × (Vs / Vp)

---

## 🔢 Substituting Values

Ns = 400 × (9.0 / 120)

---

## 🧮 Calculation

9.0 / 120 = 0.075

Ns = 400 × 0.075

Ns = 30

---

## ✅ Final Answer

🎯 **Ns = 30 turns**

---

## 💡 Interpretation

* 🔽 Voltage decreases from 120 V to 9 V → step-down transformer
* 🔄 Number of turns also decreases
* 🧵 Secondary coil has fewer turns than the primary coil

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

Voltage and number of turns are directly proportional:

* 📈 More turns → Higher voltage
* 📉 Fewer turns → Lower voltage
