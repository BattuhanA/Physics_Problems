# Question 7: Wavelength and Frequency 🌈

## 🔢 Given Data

- Wavelength:  
  λ = 550 nm = 550 × 10⁻⁹ m
- Speed of light:  
  c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s

---

## 🎨 1. What color does this wavelength correspond to?

The **visible light spectrum** ranges approximately as follows:

- Violet: 380 – 450 nm 🟣  
- Blue: 450 – 495 nm 🔵  
- Green: 495 – 570 nm 🟢  
- Yellow: 570 – 590 nm 🟡  
- Orange: 590 – 620 nm 🟠  
- Red: 620 – 750 nm 🔴  

Given:

λ = 550 nm

👉 This falls within the **495 – 570 nm** range.

✅ **Final Answer:**  
**Color = Green 🟢**

---

## ⚡ 2. Calculating the Frequency

We use the fundamental wave equation:

f = c / λ

Where:
- f = frequency (Hz)
- c = speed of light
- λ = wavelength

---

## 🧮 Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Convert wavelength

λ = 550 × 10⁻⁹ m

Rewrite in scientific notation:

550 = 5.5 × 10²  

So:

λ = 5.5 × 10² × 10⁻⁹  
λ = 5.5 × 10⁻⁷ m

---

### Step 2: Substitute into formula

f = (3 × 10⁸) / (5.5 × 10⁻⁷)

---

### Step 3: Separate numbers and powers

f = (3 / 5.5) × 10^(8 - (-7))  
f = (3 / 5.5) × 10¹⁵  

---

### Step 4: Numerical calculation

3 / 5.5 ≈ 0.545

f ≈ 0.545 × 10¹⁵  

Convert to standard scientific form:

f ≈ 5.45 × 10¹⁴ Hz ⚡

---

## ✅ 3. Final Results

- 🎨 **Color:** Green 🟢  
- ⚡ **Frequency:**  
  f ≈ 5.45 × 10¹⁴ Hz  

---

## 🧠 4. Physical Interpretation

- As **wavelength decreases**, frequency **increases** 📉📈  
- Green light lies near the **center of the visible spectrum** 🌈  
- The human eye is **most sensitive** around ~550 nm 👁️  

---

## 📌 5. Key Formula Reminder

The fundamental relationship for electromagnetic waves:

c = λ · f

- Always valid in vacuum 🚀  
- Shows how wavelength and frequency are inversely related 🔄  

---

## ✨ Summary

- 550 nm → **Green light 🟢**  
- Frequency is in the order of **10¹⁴ Hz**, typical for visible light ⚡
