# ⚡ Task 7 – Cyclotron Motion

## 🔄 Problem

An electron is accelerated from rest through a potential difference of:

\[
V = 5000 \, \text{V}
\]

It then enters a region with a uniform magnetic field:

\[
B = 0.1 \, \text{T}
\]

The magnetic field is **perpendicular** to the velocity.

---

### 🎯 Goal
Find the **radius of the circular path**.

---

## 🧠 Concept

- ⚡ Electric potential energy converts into kinetic energy  
- 🧲 Magnetic field causes circular motion  

---

## 🔹 Step 1: Convert Energy → Velocity

The electron gains kinetic energy:

\[
\frac{1}{2}mv^2 = qV
\]

Solve for velocity:

\[
v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}
\]

---

### 🔹 Substitute values

\[
q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, C
\]

\[
m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \, kg
\]

\[
v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot (1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot 5000}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}}
\]

\[
v \approx 4.2 \times 10^{7} \, \text{m/s}
\]

---

## 🔹 Step 2: Circular Motion

Magnetic force provides centripetal force:

\[
qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}
\]

Solve for radius:

\[
r = \frac{mv}{qB}
\]

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Substitute values

\[
r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}) \cdot (4.2 \times 10^7)}
{(1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot 0.1}
\]

\[
r \approx 2.4 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m}
\]

---

## ✅ Final Answer

\[
\boxed{r \approx 2.4 \, \text{mm}}
\]

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- ⚡ Higher voltage → higher speed  
- 🧲 Stronger magnetic field → smaller radius  
- 🔄 Motion becomes perfectly circular when velocity ⟂ magnetic field
