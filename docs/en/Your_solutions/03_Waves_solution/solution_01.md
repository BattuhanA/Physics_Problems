# 🌊 Wave Properties – Wavelength Calculation

## 📌 Problem
A sound wave in air has a frequency of **440 Hz**.

- Speed of sound in air: **343 m/s**
- Speed of sound in water: **1482 m/s**

👉 Find the wavelength:
1. In air  
2. In water  

---

## 🧠 Concept

The relationship between wave speed, frequency, and wavelength is:

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

Where:
- \( v \) = wave speed (m/s)  
- \( f \) = frequency (Hz)  
- \( \lambda \) = wavelength (m)  

👉 Rearranging the formula:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

---

## 🌬️ 1. Wavelength in Air

Given:
- \( v = 343 \, m/s \)
- \( f = 440 \, Hz \)

$$
\lambda_{air} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{air} \approx 0.78 \, m
$$

✅ **Wavelength in air ≈ 0.78 meters**

---

## 🌊 2. Wavelength in Water

Given:
- \( v = 1482 \, m/s \)
- \( f = 440 \, Hz \)

$$
\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{water} \approx 3.37 \, m
$$

✅ **Wavelength in water ≈ 3.37 meters**

---

## ⚡ Key Insight

- Frequency **does not change** between media  
- Speed **increases in water**  
- Therefore, wavelength **increases**

Because:

$$
\lambda \propto v
$$

---

## 🎯 Final Answers

| Medium | Speed (m/s) | Wavelength (m) |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| Air    | 343        | 0.78           |
| Water  | 1482       | 3.37           |

---

## 💡 Bonus Tip

This is why sound travels **faster and with longer wavelengths in water** compared to air.

Applications:
- Sonar systems  
- Marine communication  
- Underwater acoustics
