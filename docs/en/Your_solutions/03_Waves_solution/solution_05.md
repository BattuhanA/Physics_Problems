# Echo Ranging Experiment

## Problem
A person shouts towards a cliff and hears the echo 1 second later.  
The speed of sound in air is given as 343 m/s.  

Find the distance to the cliff.

---

## Physical Argumentation

Sound is a mechanical wave that travels through air.  
The given speed (343 m/s) means:

- In 1 second, sound travels 343 meters.

However, the measured time (1 second) is not the time taken to reach the cliff only.

It includes two parts:
1. Sound traveling from the person to the cliff  
2. Sound reflecting and traveling back to the person  

So, the total distance covered in 1 second is:
- forward distance + return distance  

---

## Logical Construction

Since the sound travels 343 meters in total during 1 second,  
and this represents a round trip:

- Total distance = 343 meters  
- One-way distance = half of total distance  

---

## Calculation

Distance to cliff = 343 / 2 = 171.5 meters

---

## Conclusion

The echo method measures round-trip travel time of sound.  
By recognizing that the sound travels to the cliff and back,  
we divide the total distance by 2 to obtain the actual distance.

**Final Answer: 171.5 meters**
