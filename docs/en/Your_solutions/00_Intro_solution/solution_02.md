# Task – System of Equations

## Problem Statement

Find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy the system:

$$
2x + 3y = 12
$$

$$
x - y = 1
$$

---

## Theory

A system of linear equations can be solved by substitution or elimination.

Here, substitution is convenient.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Express one variable

From the second equation:

$$
x - y = 1
$$

$$
x = y + 1
$$

---

### 2. Substitute into the first equation

$$
2x + 3y = 12
$$

Substitute $x = y + 1$:

$$
2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
$$

---

### 3. Simplify

$$
2y + 2 + 3y = 12
$$

$$
5y + 2 = 12
$$

$$
5y = 10
$$

$$
y = 2
$$

---

### 4. Find $x$

$$
x = y + 1
$$

$$
x = 2 + 1
$$

$$
x = 3
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
x = 3, \quad y = 2
$$

---

## Interpretation

The values $x = 3$ and $y = 2$ satisfy both equations simultaneously, meaning the two lines intersect at this point.
