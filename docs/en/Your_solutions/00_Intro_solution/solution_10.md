## Step-by-Step Solution

The motion alternates between horizontal and vertical directions.

Horizontal displacement

$$
1 - \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{5} - \frac{1}{7} + ...
$$

This is the alternating harmonic series of odd terms.

Vertical displacement

$$
\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{6} - \frac{1}{8} + ...
$$

This is also an alternating harmonic series.

Both series converge according to the alternating series test.

Therefore the total horizontal and vertical displacement approach finite limits.

## Final Result

The ant approaches a fixed position relative to the origin.

## Interpretation

Although the ant continues moving forever, the decreasing step lengths cause the total displacement to converge. The trajectory spirals toward a limiting point.
