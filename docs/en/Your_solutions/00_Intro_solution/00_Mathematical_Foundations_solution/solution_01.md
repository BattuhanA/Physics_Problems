# Task 01 – Vector Algebra

## Problem Statement
Vectors $a=[2,1,-3]$, $b=[4,-2,1]$.

## Theory
Vector magnitude

$$
|v| = \sqrt{v_1^2+v_2^2+v_3^2}
$$

Dot product

$$
a\cdot b = a_1b_1+a_2b_2+a_3b_3
$$

Angle relation

$$
\cos\theta = \frac{a\cdot b}{|a||b|}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Magnitude of $a$

$$
|a|=\sqrt{2^2+1^2+(-3)^2}
$$

$$
|a|=\sqrt{14}
$$

Magnitude of $b$

$$
|b|=\sqrt{4^2+(-2)^2+1^2}
$$

$$
|b|=\sqrt{21}
$$

Dot product

$$
a\cdot b=2(4)+1(-2)+(-3)(1)
$$

$$
=3
$$

## Final Result

$$
|a|=\sqrt{14}
$$

$$
|b|=\sqrt{21}
$$

$$
a\cdot b=3
$$

## Interpretation
Positive dot product implies an acute angle between vectors.
